Biography

Research profile

Shaoxun Liu is a Research Fellow with the College of Control Science and Engineering, Zhejiang University. He received the B.Eng. degree in Marine Engineering from Dalian Maritime University in 2017, the M.Eng. degree in Vehicle Engineering from Dalian University of Technology in 2020, and the Ph.D. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2025. His academic training spans marine power systems, vehicle engineering, robot dynamics and intelligent control, providing him with an interdisciplinary background in mechanical systems, electrical actuation and control theory.

His research focuses on the modeling, observation, control and system integration of heavy-duty embodied robotic platforms and autonomous ground vehicles. Particular interests include heavy-duty wheel-legged and closed-chain robots, hybrid-drive mobile platforms, sensorless ground-reaction-force estimation, payload and disturbance observation, servo fault diagnosis, fixed-time and fault-tolerant control, distributed-drive vehicle control, and trajectory tracking under uncertain loads and complex terrain conditions. His work emphasizes the complete research chain from dynamic modeling and controller design to simulation, test-rig validation and physical-platform experiments. He currently serves as an Associate Editor of the International Journal of Electric and Hybrid Vehicles and as a reviewer for several international journals in robotics, control and mechanical systems.

Current Research Theme

Unified modeling, observation, control and verification for heavy-duty embodied robotic platforms operating under strong coupling, uncertain loads and actuator faults.

Research Interests

Four connected research themes
01

Heavy-Duty Hybrid-Drive Robotics

Dynamic modeling, actuation coordination and motion control of wheel-legged, closed-chain and multi-actuated robotic platforms.

02

Sensorless Force and State Estimation

Ground-reaction-force estimation, payload sensing, disturbance observation and state reconstruction using actuator and inertial information.

03

Fault Diagnosis and Fault-Tolerant Control

Servo-fault diagnosis, tolerance capability assessment, proactive trajectory modification and safe degradation under actuator failures.

04

Vehicle Dynamics and Autonomous Control

Trajectory tracking, stability control, distributed-drive coordination and robust motion control of autonomous ground vehicles.
