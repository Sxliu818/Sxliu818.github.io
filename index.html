Biography

Research profile

Shaoxun Liu is a Research Fellow with the College of Control Science and Engineering, Zhejiang University. He received the B.Eng. degree in Marine Engineering from Dalian Maritime University in 2017, the M.Eng. degree in Vehicle Engineering from Dalian University of Technology in 2020, and the Ph.D. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2025. His research interests include heavy-duty wheel-legged robots, hybrid-drive mobile platforms, vehicle and robot dynamics, sensorless force estimation, disturbance observation, fault diagnosis, fault-tolerant control, and autonomous motion control.

Current Research Theme

Unified modeling, observation, control and verification for heavy-duty embodied robotic platforms operating under strong coupling, uncertain loads and actuator faults.

Research Interests

Four connected research themes
01

Heavy-Duty Hybrid-Drive Robotics

Dynamic modeling, actuation coordination and motion control of wheel-legged, closed-chain and multi-actuated robotic platforms.

02

Sensorless Force and State Estimation

Ground-reaction-force estimation, payload sensing, disturbance observation and state reconstruction using actuator and inertial information.

03

Fault Diagnosis and Fault-Tolerant Control

Servo-fault diagnosis, tolerance capability assessment, proactive trajectory modification and safe degradation under actuator failures.

04

Vehicle Dynamics and Autonomous Control

Trajectory tracking, stability control, distributed-drive coordination and robust motion control of autonomous ground vehicles.
